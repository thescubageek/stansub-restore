Mission Statement :

 To open up the deep sea frontier by making it as affordable and

safe to explore as humanly possible.

Welcome to my Website!  Here you will find information on the only gliding submarine ever built, and the world's deepest diving submersible to offer rides to the general public. The first submersible is named C-BUG- an acronym for Controlled by Buoyancy Underwater Glider. This sub took eight years to build as I was young, in school, and somewhat underfinanced at the time. It made it's first dives in May of 1997 and has made over 550 dives in 3 countries since. For two years it operated in Roatan Honduras, exploring the depths of the Cayman Trench. Children as young as 6 and senior citizens as old as 84 have been able to explore uncharted waters with me, traveling deeper and seeing things unthinkable to even professionally trained divers. Taking the lessons learned from this operation I decided to build a new submersible, since named the Idabel, after the town where it was built in Oklahoma.  It is now operating in the same location as C-BUG, at Half Moon Bay Cabins, Roatan, Honduras.  This submersible is not only "bigger and better" than C-BUG, but represents the first time in history a deep submersible has been designed for one specific dive location. It is also the first time a deep submersible has been constructed of three variable sized spheres, and is the deepest sub ever constructed by a private individual. I cannot say I did it all my own however, many individuals and businesses contributed, both in Oklahoma, and in fact from all over the world.

Many ride options are offered for those seeking journeys to "inner space". There is a shipwreck at 2600 feet to explore, a seamount raising over 2000 feet like a castle spire, a night dive option, and even an option where a couple can join the "half mile down club".  The most common option though is a 1200 foot deep dive that lasts an hour and 15 minutes. Pricing is not per person, but per trip, with a 550 pound weight allowance.  Prices start at $500 for the trip. Passengers are encouraged to bring warm clothes if desiring to go on any of the deeper dives, and any music they may like to hear on their journey, as the vehicle has a built in CD player. With the commencement of dives by Idabel, new ground is being broken for tourist submersibles. The only other sub to offer rides to the public is in the Cayman Islands and stops at 1000' and charges $450 PER PERSON for a 50 minute dive.  One's only other option for getting this deep (and back up again) is to charter a research sub with tender boat, which starts at $8,000 a day).

In addition to operating this submarine, I am available to consult people interested in building a submarine of their own. I adhere to a philosophy that no two people should ever have the same submersible. To maximize use of one's submarine and keep it from joining the many that sit in storage, it needs to be designed as specifically for it's intended use as possible.

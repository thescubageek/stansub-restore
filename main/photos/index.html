Glass Sponges are named exactly that becuase of the hard fibers they are made of. Touching one will most likely give you micro splinters which can be very painful.


photo #3

This photo is shot in all natural light. Notice the front of the sub. To get this photograph I had to sit the submarine down on a small ledge at 420 feet deep. Then with the camera bolted to the inside of the sub, and the the sub totally still, the camera shutter was held open for 2 1/2 minutes. The formations are solid limestone. This picture shows 100 feet of the wall. Horizontal visibility this day was over 200 feet.

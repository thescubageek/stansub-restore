11-1-02
After a six month treasure salvage job, I finally made a firm comitment to build a second submersible. In preparation for this I drove across the country and back taking over six months to meet withy those knowledgable in the field, attend trade shows, and gather parts. I also decided on a location to build the new sub, based on the recomendation of businessman Buck Hill, whom I met on Roatan. Idabel OK was chosen for it's low cost of living, local support/ interest in the project, and abundance of machine shops and steel suppliers in the area and a nearby deep lake suitable for testing. Since April I have been living here and working to make the new submarine come to life.

Like C-BUG, my new sub (to be named Idabel in reconigition of the eutusiasm of local residents for the project) will be innovative in many ways. Idabel is made up of three spheres, of three different sizes jioned in an L. The pilot stands in the 2 smaller ones and the third houses two passengers who this time get the foward view via a 30 inch diameter domed and 19 inch diameter flat viewports.

Idabel also reprents the first time in history a deep submersible (design depth > 1/2 mile) is being custom designed for one specific dive location. This is evident by such features as upwards looking viewports to take in the spectacular views looking up the wall, twin propulsion systems for redundancy while operating automously and battery capacity sufficent to reach the wreck of the cargo ship RV Wendy , sunk 1.6 miles from my base of operations, again Half Moon Bay Cabins.

Like C-BUG it is highly engineered to reduce displacement, however this time a greater premium was put on passenger space as refected by gross vechicle weight jumping to 7,000 lbs for 3 people vs. 2400 lbs for C-BUGS's 2 people. (still highly compact when compared to say Alvin that also carries 3 people but weighs 32,000 pounds).

By the end of this month the pressure hull will be complete and I will start outfitting it with the different systems (electrical, life support, ballast etc.) for which over 90% of the components are allready on hand. Estimated launch date is May 2003- with resumption of tourist dives in Roatan that summer. One thousand, 200 foot dives will be the base dive, with the wreck, 2,500 and night dives as options. Please consider coming to Roatan and joining me on a trip exploring inner-space!
